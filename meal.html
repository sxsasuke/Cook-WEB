THE AUTHOR OF THE RECIPE
One of the most popular desserts in the world — brownies — was invented in 1893 in the kitchen of the legendary Palmer House hotel in Chicago. This pie is still baked there according to the original recipe, covering the top with apricot glaze. In the home version, however, brownies get such an amazing sugar crust that it would be a crime to glaze it. Traditional chocolate brownies have a vanilla flavor, a crisp crust and a moist middle. Walnuts or pistachios are also added to the cake, as well as cranberries.
